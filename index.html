A doorway into the lives of women whose stories embody courage, compassion, and endurance. In the face of conflict, disaster, and hardship, they rose as leaders, storytellers, and guardians of peace.

UN Women presents this collection to honor their experiences and to inspire us all to recognize that peace is not an ideal we hope for, but a journey we must walk together.

The Women, Peace and Security (WPS) agenda is built on four main pillars:

  • Protection: Safeguarding the rights and safety of women and girls during conflict and crises.
  • Prevention: Working to stop violence and conflict before it happens, including addressing risks of gender-based violence.
  • Participation: Ensuring women have a real voice and role in peace talks, decision-making, and community efforts.
  • Relief & Recovery: Supporting women, families, and communities to heal and rebuild after conflict or disaster.

Together, these pillars highlight that lasting peace is only possible when women's experiences and leadership are fully recognized and included.

Introduction

Across generations, storytelling has been a cornerstone of culture and wisdom, a way to pass down knowledge, preserve identity, and ignite change. In Central Sulawesi, a land rich in tradition and resilience, women have long held the role of storytellers. Women are the guardians of history, peacebuilders in their communities, and voices of strength in times of crisis.

Today, their stories carry not only the weight of tradition, but also the momentum of a global movement, one that recognizes women's voices as vital - not only in honoring the strength and wisdom the past, but in shaping a more peaceful and just future.

This global movement is guided by the Women, Peace and Security (WPS) agenda - an international framework born from the landmark UN Security Council Resolution 1325 in 2000 and further strengthened by a series of follow-up resolutions. The WPS agenda recognizes the distinct and often disproportionate impacts of conflict on women and girls, while affirming that they are not only survivors of crisis, but powerful agents of change capable of transforming the very conditions that give rise to violence and paving the way toward a more inclusive and enduring peace.

Turning global commitments into local action requires dedicated partnerships, sustained advocacy, and community leadership. As the lead UN agency on advancing the WPS agenda, UN Women supports women’s meaningful participation in conflict resolution, peacebuilding, and security efforts. In Indonesia, this commitment is reflected in the National Action Plan for the Protection and Empowerment of Women and Children during Social Conflicts (NAP WPS) and the National Action Plan on Preventing and Countering Violent Extremism that Leads to Terrorism (NAP PCVE), both of which integrate gender-responsive strategies to sustain peace and resilience.

While commitments and policy frameworks lay the foundation for peace and inclusion, it is through individual stories that the true meaning of these agendas comes to life. The WPS agenda is not only written in resolutions and plans; it is lived by women in their homes, communities, and everyday acts of courage and leadership. It is with this belief that this storybook was created: to honor the voices of women who are shaping the future from the ground up.

This book brings to life the lived experiences of eight remarkable women from Central Sulawesi, each embodying the spirit of peace and resilience. Their stories are woven into the fabric of the WPS agenda and are organized around its four pillars that are the guiding principles that frame both the challenges women face in conflict and the roles they play in building peace.

Participation

Women play a meaningful role in decision- making processes related to peace and security, from grassroots initiatives to national and international policymaking.

Protection

Safeguarding women and girls from gender-based violence and human rights violations, especially in conflict and post-conflict settings.

Prevention

Address the root causes of conflict and violence by promoting gender equality, strengthening early warning systems, and preventing the spread of violent extremism.

Relief & Recovery

Women's needs and leadership are central in humanitarian response, post-conflict reconstruction, and peacebuilding efforts.

Through their lived experiences, these women illuminate the challenges and triumphs of advocating for peace in the face of conflict and disaster. Their journeys underscore the undeniable link between gender equality and sustainable peace, demonstrating that when women lead, communities thrive. Many of these stories are rooted in the historical backdrop of the Poso conflict, earthquake and recovery, climate change and the ongoing movement of women-led organizations to integrate women’s leadership in peacebuilding processes.

This book is more than a collection of stories; it is a call to action. To the general public, it offers a window into the resilience and leadership of women in peace processes. To donors and policymakers, it is a testament to why investing in women is investing in peace. To government counterparts, it serves as a reminder that empowering women is not just an aspiration; it is a necessity.

With support from the Government of the Netherlands and Korea International Cooperation Agency (KOICA), UN Women continues to enhance the leadership skills of women and women's organizations in Central Sulawesi and beyond, highlighting their potential as agents of peace.

As you turn these pages, may you be moved by the courage, wisdom, and unwavering hope of these women storytellers. In honoring their stories, we also honor the generations of women who came before them, and the generations of peacebuilders they now inspire. Their voices are an invitation to listen, to learn, and to act because peace is not just an ideal, but a shared responsibility.
